About

Welcome to my webpage! I am a fifth year PhD Student in Economics at the University of Maryland College Park. My main research interests are in International Macroeconomics and Trade with a focus on propagation and aggregation.

Update: I'll be on the Job Market in the academic year 2023 -- 2024! Stay tuned for more updates and feel free to reach me via email at asilvub at umd dot edu or asilvub at gmail dot com.

with Jorge Miranda-Pinto and Eric R. Young
Journal of Monetary Economics, Forthcoming

Quantifying the Inflationary Impact of Fiscal Stimulus under Supply Constraints

with Julian di Giovanni, Şebnem Kalemli-Özcan, and Muhammed A. Yıldırım
AEA Papers and Proceedings, 113, 76 -- 80, 2023
Fiscal stimulus in the US account for half of the aggregate demand effect on US inflation between December 2019 -- June 2022.
  • Other versions: Local PDF, CEPR WP, NBER WP, NYFED WP.

Global Supply Chain Pressures, International Trade, and Inflation

with Julian di Giovanni, Şebnem Kalemli-Özcan, and Muhammed A. Yıldırım
Conference Proceedings ECB Forum: "Challenges for Monetary Policy in a Rapidly Changing World", 2022
Supply forces are more important in explaining inflation in the Euro Area than in the US during 2019-2021. Global supply chains limited the increase in international trade.

  • Other Versions: NBER WP, CEPR WP, NYFED WP
  • Media Coverage: Bloomberg, The Economist, Financial Times, NYFED Blog, VOXEU.
  • Video: SINTRA Sebnem's Presentation
  • Slides: NBER IFM Meeting Fall 2022

Impact of Economic Uncertainty in a Small Open Economy: The Case of Chile

with Rodrigo Cerda and Jose Tomas Valente
